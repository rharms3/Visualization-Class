Crime Across the United States Analysis:

We will be looking at the crime statistics from across the US from 1998 – 2018 provided by FBI UCR (FBI Uniform Crime Reporting System).

"The FBI’s Uniform Crime Reporting (UCR) Program serves as the national repository for crime data collected by law enforcement. Its primary objective is to generate reliable information for use in law enforcement administration, operation, and management. Considered a leading social indicator, UCR data is used for monitoring fluctuations in crime levels, evaluating policies, and regulating staffing levels." – FBI UCR site

Scatter Plot Distribution:

The scatter plot shows distribution of total crime per state per year over the 20 year period 1998 to 2018 in the United States. The default view contains all states for the 20 year period.

  • Top three states with crime rates are California, Texas & Florida.
  • We have used log scale to provide a better split of the data per state.
  • Some of the highest crime rates are found in the most populated states in the US.

For additional details on any one state or year, one can use the Drop Down selectors for Year or State to reduce the scatter plot output. There is also the ability to hover over any plot to activate an additional information popup.

For a deeper breakdown of the types or crimes and their associated numbers, one can click on the scatter plot points to activate an additional bar graph with the detailed breakdown. On this breakdown, more details are available when hovering over the individual bars.
